## Understanding Height Mechanics in Locked 2

Height is perhaps the most critical factor when constructing your character. In Locked 2, your height isn't just cosmetic; it changes your hitbox, your jump height, and even the types of dribbling moves you can effectively execute. Players generally gravitate toward two extremes: the "Small Speedster" (5'3" - 5'6") and the "Tall Powerhouse" (6'3"+).

| Attribute | Short Build (5'3" - 5'6") | Tall Build (6'3"+) |
|---|---|---|
| **Dribbling Style** | **Flick Dribble** focus | **Chest Tap** focus |
| **Movement Speed** | High agility and acceleration | Slower startup, high reach |
| **Aerial Game** | Relies on timing | Dominates with height reach |
| **Best Ability** | Speed Burst / Shifty | **Bunnies** |

> **Warning:** Choosing a height in the middle (5'8" - 6'0") can sometimes leave you in a "dead zone" where you aren't fast enough for advanced flicks but aren't tall enough for easy chest taps without specific abilities.

## The Speedster: 5'3" Flick Dribble Build

For many competitive players, the **locked 2 best build** for offensive play involves a minimum height setting of 5'3". This build excels at the "Flick Dribble," which is currently considered the most advanced and difficult-to-defend move in the game. 

### Why 5'3" is Meta
At this height, your center of gravity allows for rapid camera-based flicks. By aiming your camera slightly upward and using your directional inputs, you can pop the ball over defenders' heads and catch it before they can react. This build is particularly effective in 1v1 situations where you need to navigate through tight spaces.

### Essential Dribbling Moves for Short Builds
1. **The Flick Dribble:** Aim the camera up and use your M1/M2 combos to pop the ball.
2. **The Chop:** A fundamental move using M2 to change direction instantly.
3. **Directional Mixups:** Using "Flick Back" to bait a defender into a lunge, then exploding forward.

## Advanced Dribbling Techniques

Regardless of your chosen **locked 2 best build**, mastering the "Legacy Dribbling" system is vital. The game uses a physics-based ball system where your camera angle and M2 timing determine the ball's trajectory.

### The Chop and Air Chop
The "Chop" is your bread and butter. By clicking M2 while moving, you "chop" the ball in the direction you are facing. To perform an "Air Chop," you simply tilt your camera upward during the animation. This lifts the ball off the ground, allowing you to bypass low-block tackles.

### Advanced Mixups
To keep defenders guessing, you should never use the same dribble twice in a row. A common high-level sequence involves:
*   **Step 1:** Perform a ground chop to the left.
*   **Step 2:** Immediately flick the camera back and perform an air chop.
*   **Step 3:** Use a directional flick to bring the ball forward while the defender is still recovering from the previous move.

> **Tip:** If you find yourself facing multiple defenders (scrimmage situations), use the "Pull Back" flick. By flicking the ball and immediately pulling your movement key backward, you can bait defenders into jumping for a ball that is no longer there.

## Strategy for Scrimmages and Matches

When playing in a team environment, your **locked 2 best build** should complement your teammates. A balanced team usually consists of one 5'3" speedster for playmaking and one 6'3" powerhouse for finishing and aerial defense.

### Defensive Counter-Play
If you are defending against a "Flick Dribbler," do not commit to the first move. Flick dribblers rely on you jumping or lunging early. Stay grounded and wait for the ball to reach its peak height before attempting a chest tap or a tackle. If you are a tall build, use your reach to block the "Air Chop" lanes.

### Offensive Transition
For 6'3" builds, the goal is to get the ball into the air as quickly as possible. Use a "Lift" (aiming the camera all the way up and clicking) to start your chain. Once the ball is at chest height, jump and use your forward momentum to carry the ball toward the goal.
