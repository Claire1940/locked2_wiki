## Tall Builds: Aerial Dominance and Tall Flicks

If you prefer a more physical presence on the field, tall **locked 2 builds** (6'3" and above) are currently very powerful. These builds specialize in keeping the ball high in the air, where shorter defenders simply cannot reach it.

### The Tall Flick Technique
The hallmark of a tall build is the "Tall Flick." To execute this, look up at a slight angle (either left or right) and kick the ball. As the ball rises, jump and use your M1 to chest trap it at its highest point. When timed correctly, the ball stays positioned directly above your head. Because of your height, any defender shorter than you will find it nearly impossible to intercept the ball without fouling or missing the hitbox entirely.

### Self-Lobbing
Self-lobbing is a simplified version of the tall flick. By looking directly up, jumping, and clicking M1, you can brute-force your way down the field. This is particularly effective against defenders who do not have the "Bunnies" trait, as they won't have the vertical leap required to challenge your possession.

## Small Builds: Agility and Flick Dribbling

Smaller **locked 2 builds** are designed for players who want to break ankles and use pure speed to outmaneuver the opposition. While they lack the aerial reach of taller players, their ground game is significantly more complex.

*   **Flick Dribbling:** Small builds can kick the ball at sharp angles and recover it faster than taller builds. This allows for rapid "zig-zag" movements that are difficult for defenders to track.
*   **Mini Flicks:** By looking straight at the ground while dribbling, you can perform a mini flick. This is a subtle move that can bait a defender into a slide tackle, leaving them out of position while you accelerate past them.
*   **Header Combos:** Headers are more reliable on shorter builds because they don't restrict your jump recovery as much as they do on taller characters. You can flick the ball, header it to maintain a low trajectory, and immediately jump again for a follow-up strike.

## Advanced Lift Dribbling Mechanics

Regardless of which **locked 2 builds** you choose, mastering the "Lift Dribble" is the key to high-level play. This mechanic involves clicking the ball upward and then "catching" it with your M1 at the absolute peak of its arc.

1.  **Initiate the Lift:** Click the ball to send it upward (either straight or at an angle).
2.  **The Peak Click:** Wait for the ball to reach its highest point before clicking M1 again.
3.  **Camera Control:** Once the ball is "lifted," use your camera to steer. Tilting your view left and right while moving forward allows you to weave through the defense while keeping the ball in a protected state.

> **Warning:** Avoid spamming the lift dribble too predictably. Experienced defenders will time their jumps to intercept the ball at its peak if you don't vary your timing and direction.

## Strategic Mixups and Fake Shots

To truly excel with your chosen build, you must incorporate mixups into your gameplay. The "Fake Shot" is a scenario-based tool that works exceptionally well in 1v1 situations near the goal. By initiating a shot animation and then canceling or pivoting, you can force the goalkeeper or defender to commit to a block, opening up a clear lane for a real strike.

Another effective mixup is the "Chop Dribble" (M2). This allows you to suddenly change the ball's momentum, which is especially effective when combined with the high-speed flick dribbling of a small build. By alternating between high-altitude lobs and low-altitude chops, you keep the defense guessing and unable to settle into a rhythm.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the best height for a beginner in Locked 2?**

A: For new players, a tall build (6'3" or taller) is often recommended. These **locked 2 builds** allow for a simpler dribbling experience through lobs and tall flicks, which are easier to execute and harder for opponents to defend against compared to high-speed ground dribbling.

**Q: Does weight affect my build as much as height?**

A: While height is the primary factor for ball interaction and hitboxes, weight can subtly influence your momentum and how you interact with other players during collisions. However, height remains the most impactful stat for dribbling mechanics.

**Q: Is the Legacy dribble style really better than Beginner?**

A: Yes, for players looking to improve. Legacy style pushes the ball ahead of you, which provides the necessary space to perform lifts, lobs, and advanced flicks. Beginner style is safer for basic possession but has a much lower skill ceiling.

**Q: How do I counter a tall build user who keeps lobbing the ball?**

A: The best way to counter tall builds is to use the "Bunnies" trait if available, or to time your jump so that you meet the ball at the same time as the attacker. Additionally, staying close and preventing them from having the space to look up and initiate the lob is a key defensive strategy.
