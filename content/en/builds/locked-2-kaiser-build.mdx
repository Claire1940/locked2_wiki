## Weapon Synergy: Emperor and Riptide

The "Broken" nature of the Kaiser Magnus setup comes from the interplay between your two weapon slots. In Locked 2, your secondary weapon often provides passive buffs or specific active skills that complement your primary playstyle.

### The Riptide Advantage
Equipping Riptide in your first slot is essential for maintaining momentum. Riptide excels in transition play, allowing you to "run the line" and navigate through tight marking from defenders. Its ability to maintain ball glue while moving at high speeds makes it the perfect tool for a Striker who needs to create their own space.

### The Emperor's Reach
In the second slot, Emperor acts as the finisher. This weapon is synonymous with the "Kaiser" identity, providing the technical stats needed to pull off curved shots and high-velocity strikes. When you are in the box, switching to or utilizing the passives from Emperor ensures that your shots have the necessary "Magnus" effect to bypass elite goalkeepers.

## Physical Stats and Height Optimization

Height is a frequently debated stat in Locked 2, but for the **locked 2 kaiser build**, the consensus for 2026 is a height of **6'3"**. This height provides a "sweet spot" for several reasons:

1.  **Aerial Dominance:** At 6'3", you can out-jump smaller wingers and challenge center-backs for headers during crosses.
2.  **Stride Length:** Longer legs translate to a larger hitbox for intercepting passes and a slightly longer reach when dribbling.
3.  **Speed Retention:** Unlike maximum height builds (which can feel sluggish), 6'3" maintains enough agility to perform quick turns in the box.

### Stat Priority for Strikers
When allocating points or choosing gear to complement your Kaiser build, focus on the following hierarchy:

| Priority | Stat | Reason |

## Tactical Execution and Positioning

Having the right build is only half the battle; knowing how to utilize the **locked 2 kaiser build** in a live match is what separates experts from novices. As a Striker using this setup, your positioning should be dynamic rather than static.

### Occupying the Half-Spaces
Don't just sit in the middle of the box. Use the Riptide's mobility to occupy the "half-spaces" between the opponent's full-backs and center-backs. This forces the defense to make a choice: leave you open or pull a defender out of position, creating a gap for your teammates.

### Calling for the Cross
The Kaiser build shines during direct play. When your wingers have the ball, look for the "Circle" or "Box" calls. Your 6'3" frame allows you to receive high balls or low, hard crosses with a high success rate. Because you have Emperor equipped, even a poorly angled pass can be converted into a goal through sheer technical stat overmatch.

> **💡 Tip:** Always keep an eye on your "Flow" meter. Activating Flow while using the Emperor weapon essentially guarantees a goal-scoring opportunity if you have a clear line of sight.

## Defensive Responsibilities and Stealthy Play

Even as a Striker, you cannot ignore the defensive side of the game. In modern Locked 2 play, "Stealthy" mechanics are often used to mask movements. Ensure your team is communicating who has "Stealthy" active so you can position yourself in their blind spots.

If the opposition is crowding the box with four or more players, use your build's technical prowess to play back. Passing the ball to a trailing midfielder can draw the defense out, allowing you to use your speed to reset and find a new opening.

## Advanced Techniques for the Kaiser Build

To truly push the **locked 2 kaiser build** to its limits, you should practice the following maneuvers in the training hub before heading into competitive matches:

*   **The Riptide Reset:** Use a quick move to drop the ball back to a midfielder and immediately sprint into the box. The speed boost from Riptide makes this "give and go" extremely hard to track.
*   **Emperor's Curve:** Practice shooting from the corners of the penalty area. The Kaiser build is designed to exploit the angles that standard builds cannot reach.
*   **Box Boxing:** Use your 6'3" frame to physically shield the ball from defenders while waiting for your teammates to arrive in support.

## FAQ

**Q: Is the locked 2 kaiser build viable for solo queue?**

A: Yes, the build is highly viable for solo play because it provides you with the tools to create your own shots. However, it performs significantly better when you have a teammate who can provide consistent crosses.

**Q: Why 6'3" instead of 6'5" for this build?**

A: While 6'5" offers more height, the 6'3" height provides better lateral quickness. In the 2026 meta, being able to turn quickly in the box is more valuable than the extra two inches of height, especially when using the Riptide weapon.

**Q: Can I use a different weapon instead of Riptide?**

A: You can, but you may lose the specific "broken" synergy that makes the **locked 2 kaiser build** so effective. Riptide provides the necessary mobility that Emperor lacks, creating a perfectly balanced offensive kit.

**Q: How do I counter a Kaiser build defender?**

A: The best way to counter this build is through physical pressure. If you can prevent the Striker from finding space to use their Emperor weapon, you neutralize their greatest threat. Using a high-strength defender build is usually the most effective strategy.
