## Understanding Height Archetypes in Locked 2

In Locked 2, height is generally categorized into two main archetypes: Small Heights and Tall Heights. Each has a distinct "feel" and specific mechanics that they excel at. While many veteran players from the original game have personal preferences, the sequel has introduced nuances that make certain heights objectively better for specific roles.

### Small Builds (5'3" to 6'3")
Small builds are the gold standard for players who want to focus on technical dribbling and high-speed mix-ups. At these heights, the ball stays closer to your center of gravity during ground dribbles, and your recovery time after certain animations is significantly faster.

*   **Flick Specialization:** Small heights are built for flick dribbling. Because they are lower to the ground, the angle at which they kick the ball allows for more consistent follow-ups.
*   **Header Reliability:** Unlike taller builds, smaller players do not suffer from severe jump restrictions after performing a header, making them surprisingly effective at aerial combos.
*   **Agility:** Navigating through tight defensive lines is much easier when your hitbox is compact.

### Tall Builds (Above 6'3")
Tall builds are often preferred by defenders or players who want a "simpler" but highly effective offensive game. They rely on height-based mechanics that shorter players simply cannot contest.

*   **Tall Flicks:** This is the most "overpowered" mechanic for tall players. By flicking the ball high and chest-trapping it, you can keep the ball at an elevation where shorter defenders cannot reach it.
*   **Self-Lobbing:** Taller builds can brute force their way down the field by looking directly up and jumping to click the ball, creating a path that is difficult to intercept.
*   **Reach:** Defensively, a taller build covers more ground, making it easier to block shots and intercept passes.

## Advanced Dribbling Mechanics by Height

Your choice of the **best height in locked 2** will determine which of these advanced techniques you should focus on mastering.

### Flick Dribbling (Best for Small Heights)
To execute a proper flick dribble, you must look at a slight angle to the left or right and kick the ball. As the ball begins its descent, you must run toward it and click (or auto-dribble). Small builds excel here because they can maintain the momentum of the ball without it flying too far out of reach.

### Tall Flicks and Chest Traps (Best for Tall Heights)
This is the bread and butter of tall builds. Look up at an angle, kick the ball, and then jump to meet it at its highest point. By using M1 to chest trap the ball, you create a "safe zone." If you are taller than the defender, they literally cannot reach the ball if your timing is correct. This makes it one of the most reliable ways to move the ball toward the goal.

## Optimizing Your Settings for Your Height Build

Regardless of your height, your settings must be optimized to ensure your inputs are registered correctly. High FPS and low latency are vital for the timing-heavy nature of Locked 2.

### Visual and Performance Settings
*   **Flow/Visual Effects:** Set to Low. Even on high-end PCs, Locked 2 can suffer from FPS drops which ruin dribbling timing.
*   **Camera Shake:** Off. This is mandatory for maintaining a clear view of the ball's arc during flicks.
*   **Sprint Style:** Set to **Hold**. This allows for much finer control over your movement speed during lift dribbles.

### Dribbling Styles
You have two choices: **Legacy** and **Beginner**.
*   **Legacy Dribbles:** These push the ball ahead of you. This is widely considered the superior choice for high-skill players because the potential for creative plays is much higher.
*   **Beginner Dribbles:** These pull the ball toward you. While easier for new players, they limit your ability to perform long-distance flicks.

| Setting | Recommended Value | Reason |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Dribble Style** | **Legacy** | Higher skill ceiling and better flick potential. |
| **Hold Dribble** | **On** | Allows for easier auto-dribbling sequences. |

| **Sprint Style** | **Hold** | Precise control over movement bursts. |

## Dribble Mix-ups and Combat Strategies

Once you have settled on the **best height in locked 2** for your style, you need to incorporate mix-ups to keep defenders guessing. Defending in the 2026 meta is notoriously difficult, so unpredictability is your best weapon.

### The Mini Flick
By looking straight at the ground while dribbling and using M2, you can perform a "mini flick." This is incredibly confusing for defenders who are expecting a high lift or a standard ground dribble. It can also be used to set up powerful shots like "Emperor," as the slight lift can sometimes boost the shot's power.

### Header Combos
Headers are a versatile tool. While they are more reliable on shorter builds (due to less jump restriction), they can be used by any height to trap a ball or continue a combo. If you are defending, you can slide into the ball, shoot it upward, and then header it to a teammate to quickly clear the zone.

### Fake Shots
This is a scenario-based move. If you find yourself in a 1v1 or 2v1 situation near the net, a fake shot can force the defender to commit to a block, leaving them out of position when you actually go for the goal.

> **💡 Tip:** Practice "Lift Dribbles" by clicking the ball at its highest peak. Mastering this single mechanic will make you nearly unstoppable, regardless of your height, as it is the foundation for almost every advanced move in the game.

## FAQ

**Q: Is there a single best height in Locked 2 for all players?**

A: No, the **best height in locked 2** depends entirely on your playstyle. If you want high-skill technical dribbling, go for a small build (5'3" - 6'3"). If you want to dominate with physical reach and "unstealable" tall flicks, choose a tall build (6'4"+).

**Q: Does height affect my running speed?**

A: Height primarily affects your hitbox, reach, and the physics of the ball during dribbles. While taller builds might feel slower due to larger animations, the primary difference is in how you handle the ball and your aerial capabilities.

**Q: Can I change my height after creating my character?**

A: Yes, you can usually adjust your build through the in-game customization menu, though it may require specific currency or items depending on the current game version.

**Q: Why do I keep losing the ball to "Bunnies" users?**

A: "Bunnies" is a powerful trait for jumping. If you are a tall build and you aren't timing your chest traps perfectly at the ball's peak, a player with the Bunnies trait will be able to jump and steal the ball from you. Use "Tall Flicks" instead of simple lobs to counter this.
