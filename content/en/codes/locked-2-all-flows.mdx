## S-Tier: The Meta-Defining Flows

To truly master **locked 2 all flows**, one must understand why the S-tier entries sit at the top. These weapons offer "I-frames" (invincibility frames) or massive hitboxes that cannot be easily countered by standard gameplay mechanics.

### Emperor Impact (Unique)
Based on the legendary Kaiser from the source material, this is the premier striking weapon. When activated, your next shot becomes hyper-powered. In the 2026 meta, this is often paired with Riptide or Power Shot to create "Magna Shots" that are virtually impossible for standard goalkeepers to track. It is equally effective in both Autos and Scrims.

### Serpent (Unique)
If you are playing a defensive role, Serpent is your holy grail. Its hitbox is arguably the largest in the game, allowing you to clear out offensive threats just by popping the ability near the shooter. It was famously used to dominate the Content Creator Cup, proving that a team with multiple Serpent users is nearly impossible to score against.

### Bunnies (Rare)
Despite its lower rarity, Bunnies is a staple in competitive Scrims. When a ball is lobbed into the box, a player using Bunnies will almost always win the header or chest trap. This creates uncontested shot opportunities that can turn the tide of a 50-minute match.

## Understanding Competitive vs. Casual Play

The viability of **locked 2 all flows** changes significantly depending on the environment. In "Autos," you are often playing against less coordinated teams where raw power (like Crushing Header) can result in easy goals. However, in "Scrims," players are much more adept at timing their tackles and jumps.

1. **Scrims (Competitive):** Weapons like Bunnies and Serpent are king here. Organized teams rely on lobs and tactical positioning, making aerial dominance and large defensive hitboxes essential.
2. **Autos (Casual):** Speed and flashy dribbling weapons like Immense Speed or Big Bang Drive shine here. You can often outrun or out-dribble defenders who aren't used to high-level mechanics.
3. **The Chameleon Factor:** The legendary Chameleon weapon is a wild card. It copies a random offensive weapon if you have the ball, or a defensive one if you don't. It is the most versatile flow in the game but relies heavily on RNG.

## How to Manage Your Weapons

Since money is scarce, you must be tactical with your spins. If you land an A-tier or S-tier weapon, stop spinning immediately—even if it isn't your "favorite." 

*   **The 3,000 Rule:** If you are down to your last 3,000 in-game currency, keep whatever weapon you have unless it is in the "Terrible" tier (Monster Lift or Stealthy Steps). 
*   **Positional Locking:** If you get a top-tier defensive weapon like Serpent but want to play striker, consider changing your role rather than spinning off the weapon. High-tier uniques are too rare to discard lightly.
*   **Unique Limitations:** Remember that some weapons, like Perfect Counter, have limitations in Scrims (such as not being allowed to enter the penalty box while active) because they are considered too overpowered.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the single best weapon for scoring goals in Locked 2?**

A: Currently, **Emperor Impact** is considered the best for scoring. Its ability to hyper-power any shot makes it extremely difficult for goalkeepers to block, especially when timed correctly or combined with a curve.

**Q: Why is Metavision ranked so low in the locked 2 all flows tier list?**

A: While Metavision is a "Unique" rarity, its benefits (speed boost and ball trajectory) have a long cooldown and short duration. In competitive Scrims, other uniques like Serpent or Emperor Impact provide much more tangible game-winning advantages.

**Q: Can I use Primal Handstand as a defender?**

A: In casual Autos, you can technically use it, but in competitive Scrims, there is often a "gentleman's agreement" or specific league rules that forbid defenders from using Primal Handstand due to its broken hitbox. It is intended strictly as a goalkeeper weapon.

**Q: What are I-frames and why do they matter?**

A: I-frames stand for Invincibility Frames. When an ability grants I-frames, the ball glows blue, and it becomes impossible for an opponent to steal the ball from you using a standard tackle (M2). This allows you to reposition or line up a shot without fear of losing possession.
