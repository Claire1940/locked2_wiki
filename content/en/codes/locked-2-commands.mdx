## Accessing the Private Server Menu

Before you can start inputting commands, you need to know where to find the interface. Unlike some games that rely purely on text-based chat commands, this title utilizes a robust UI-driven system. To access the command suite, you must be the owner of a private server or have been granted admin permissions by the owner.

Once you are in your server, navigate to the **Main Menu** and look for the **Commands** tab. This is the hub for all administrative actions. If you are looking to play with specific people before diving into the commands, ensure your matchmaking region is set correctly under the "Other" tab. You can choose from North America, South America, Europe, Asia, and Oceania to ensure the lowest possible latency for your participants.

> **💡 Tip:** If you are being spammed with party invites while trying to set up your server, you can toggle invite notifications off in the "Other" settings menu to maintain your focus.

## Essential Locked 2 Commands for Server Owners

The core of server management lies in the "Match" and "Server" toggles. These allow you to set the ground rules for your session. For example, the **Server Lock** command is your first line of defense against unwanted players joining mid-match.

| Command | Category | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Server Lock** | Server | Prevents new players from joining the instance. |
| **Boss Spawning** | Gameplay | Toggles the ability for bosses to appear in the session. |
| **Cool downs** | Gameplay | Removes or enables ability cooldowns for practice. |
| **Reset Ball** | Match | Instantly teleports the ball back to the center of the pitch. |
| **Give Flow** | Player | Grants a specific player "Flow" state for testing or training. |

One of the most frequently asked questions regarding **locked 2 commands** is how to actually get the ball onto the pitch. On PC, the default keybind to spawn the ball is **B**. This is a quick-action command that bypasses the menu, allowing for rapid resets during drills.

## Match Management and Scoreboard Tools

Running an organized match requires precise control over the clock and the score. The "Match" section of the command menu provides several tools that act as a virtual referee. You can start or pause the game clock, trigger the whistle sound effect for fouls or out-of-bounds plays, and even force a kickoff.

The scoreboard is also fully interactive. If a goal is scored during a warm-up and you want to clear it before the official match begins, you can use the **Reset Scoreboard** function. Additionally, you can manually toggle the scoreboard's visibility to keep the screen clean for cinematic recordings.

### Field Customization
Field size plays a massive role in the meta of the game. Smaller fields favor high-intensity, short-passing games, while large fields are better for testing long-range shots and stamina management. Through the command menu, you can toggle between **Small** and **Large** field versions instantly.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Changing the field size mid-match can sometimes cause players or the ball to glitch through the floor. It is best to set the field size before the players spawn in or during a full match reset.

## Player Management and Administration

When hosting a public or semi-private event, you may encounter players who do not follow the rules. The player management section of the **locked 2 commands** menu gives you several ways to handle individual participants. By clicking on a player's name in the list, you can access a sub-menu with the following options:

| Action | Result |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Admin** | Grants the player access to the command menu. |
| **Referee Mode** | Puts the player in a neutral state to observe and officiate. |
| **Kick** | Removes the player from the current session. |
| **Blacklist** | Bans the player from re-joining the server. |
| **Teleport** | Moves the player to your current location. |
| **Freeze** | Roots the player in place, preventing movement. |

These tools are vital for maintaining order. For instance, if you are conducting a tutorial, "Freezing" the students ensures everyone is listening and not running around the pitch.

## Advanced Modifiers and AI Bots

For players who want to practice their offensive maneuvers without needing a full team of ten, the **AI Bot** commands are a game-changer. You can spawn an AI Goalkeeper (GK) to guard the net while you practice your shooting angles.

To place an AI GK, follow these steps:
1. Walk to the position where you want the bot to stand (usually the center of the goal line).
2. Open the **Main Menu** and navigate to **Bots**.
3. Select **Place**.
4. The AI will appear and begin defending the goal immediately.

Furthermore, you can experiment with "Multipliers." In the world of **locked 2 commands**, multipliers act as modifiers that can change physics, speed, or power levels. These are perfect for creating "mutator" game modes that provide a break from the standard competitive grind.

## Team Creation and Customization

Locked 2 allows for deep team-based customization directly through the command interface. You are not limited to the default colors or names. By clicking the "+" icon in the Team Selection menu, you can create a custom team name (e.g., "Priority" or "Elite Strikers"). 

Customization also extends to clothing. The game pulls your current Roblox avatar's clothing (minus bulky accessories) to create your kit. However, if you want to create a specific look for your team, you can use hair accessories and certain masks that the game allows to persist, giving your squad a unique identity on the pitch.

## Cinematic and Social Features

For content creators, the **Free Roam** camera is perhaps the most powerful tool in the arsenal. By pressing **Ctrl + Shift + P**, you can detach the camera from your character. This allows you to fly around the stadium, capture cinematic angles of goals, or watch the match from the sidelines like a spectator.

This feature is often used in conjunction with the "Referee Mode" to provide a broadcast-style view of competitive matches. It is a staple for anyone looking to record high-quality footage for social media or tournament highlights.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I spawn the ball in a private server?**

A: If you are on PC, simply press the **B** key. Alternatively, you can navigate to the Commands menu and select the "Reset Ball" option to bring it to the center of the field.

**Q: Can I give other players access to the locked 2 commands?**

A: Yes. In the player list within the command menu, select the player's name and click the "Admin" button. This will allow them to use the same tools as the server owner.

**Q: What is the benefit of the AI GK bot?**

A: The AI GK is an excellent tool for solo practice. It allows strikers to practice their finishing and "Flow" usage without needing another player to sit in the goal.

**Q: How do I change the matchmaking region?**

A: Go to the "Other" tab in the Main Menu and click on "Matchmaking Region." You can choose between North America, South America, Europe, Asia, and Oceania to optimize your connection.
