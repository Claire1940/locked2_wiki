## S-Tier: The Meta-Definers

The weapons in S-Tier are considered essential for high-level play. If you roll one of these, you should almost never "spin off" of it unless you are looking for a very specific niche build.

### Emperor Impact
Widely regarded as the strongest offensive weapon in the game, Emperor Impact provides a hyper-powered shot that is difficult for any goalkeeper to track. Based on the legendary Kaiser from the source material, this unique weapon can be stacked with other abilities like Riptide or Power Shot to create a "Magna Shot" that is virtually unblockable if aimed correctly.

### Serpent
For defenders, Serpent is the gold standard. Its massive hit-box allows you to clear the ball and intercept shots with ease. In competitive 2026 tournaments, teams that stack Serpent defenders often see a much higher win rate due to the weapon's ability to shut down even the most aggressive strikers.

### Bunnies
While Serpent handles the ground, Bunnies dominates the air. This weapon is a staple for fullbacks in Scrims. When a ball is lobbed into the box, a player using Bunnies will almost always win the contest, leading to a free, uncontested clearance or a counter-attack opportunity.

## A-Tier: High Utility and Versatility

These weapons are excellent choices that can compete with S-Tier options when used by skilled players. They often require more timing or specific team setups to reach their full potential.

### Immense Speed & Riptide Curve
Immense Speed allows for incredible bursts that can catch defenders off guard or help a wing-back track back to stop a breakaway. Riptide Curve, on the other hand, is the favorite of technical strikers. While it doesn't have the raw power of Emperor Impact on its own, the ability to curve shots around goalkeepers makes it a nightmare to defend in Auto matches.

### Black Hole Trap (Nagi Trap)
This epic weapon is perfect for setting up highlight-reel goals. It auto-locks the ball to the user, allowing you to jump over sliding defenders and transition immediately into a shot. In the **locked 2 tier list**, it remains a high A-tier choice for those who prefer a "flashy" playstyle that still yields results.

## B and C Tier: Niche and Situational

Weapons in these tiers are not necessarily "bad," but they are often outclassed by higher-tier alternatives or have significant drawbacks like long charge times.

*   **Power Shot:** Great for clearing the ball from the defensive third, but the long charge time makes it a liability for strikers who are under constant pressure.
*   **Perfect Counter:** A unique weapon that relies entirely on your opponent making a mistake. If they don't attempt to steal (M2) while the counter is active, the ability is wasted.
*   **Metavision:** While it provides a speed boost and shows ball trajectory, its long cooldown and short duration make it the weakest of the unique weapons in 2026.

## Goalkeeper Specific Weapons

Goalkeeping in Locked 2 is a specialized role, and your weapon choice should reflect that. There are two primary options that every keeper should consider.

1.  **Primal Handstand:** This is currently considered "broken" due to its massive hit-box. Even if a shot is headed for the top corner, a well-timed Primal Handstand can often block it from the center of the goal.
2.  **Bear Claw:** A solid common-tier weapon that provides a reliable block. It is the baseline for any aspiring goalkeeper.

> **⚠️ Warning:** In many competitive "Scrim" leagues, the use of Primal Handstand by non-goalkeepers is banned because the defensive hit-box is too large for fair field play.

## Weapon Synergy and Stacking

One of the most advanced aspects of the **locked 2 tier list** is how weapons interact with one another. High-level players don't just look at a weapon in isolation; they look at how it complements their second ability or their teammates' loadouts.

*   **The Magna Shot:** Combining **Emperor Impact** with **Riptide Curve** or **Power Shot**. This creates a shot with maximum velocity and unpredictable movement.
*   **The Aerial Wall:** Having one defender with **Bunnies** and another with **Serpent** ensures that the box is protected against both ground crosses and high lobs.
*   **The Chameleon Wildcard:** Since the Chameleon weapon copies an offensive weapon when you have the ball and a defensive one when you don't, it is the most versatile (though unpredictable) legendary in the game.

## FAQ

**Q: Which weapon should I aim for as a beginner in the Locked 2 tier list?**

A: For beginners, **Emperor Impact** is the most rewarding offensive weapon, while **Serpent** is the easiest to use for defense. Both provide immediate value without requiring complex timing or specific team compositions.

**Q: Why is Monster Lift ranked so low?**

A: Monster Lift lacks Invincibility Frames (I-frames). In competitive play, a defender can simply jump up and "chest trap" the ball away from you while you are mid-animation, making the move more of a liability than an asset.

**Q: How often does the locked 2 tier list change?**

A: The meta usually shifts with every major balance patch or when new weapons are introduced. In 2026, we have seen several "Unique" weapons receive cooldown adjustments that have moved them between A and B tiers.

**Q: Is Chameleon worth keeping?**

A: Chameleon is a high-risk, high-reward legendary. It can copy the best weapons on the field, but it can also copy "Stealthy Steps" or other low-tier abilities. If you enjoy a versatile playstyle and have the luck to pull it, it is worth keeping for the fun factor alone.
