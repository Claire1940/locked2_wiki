## S-Tier: The Meta-Defining Weapons

Weapons in this category are considered "must-haves." They offer mechanics that are difficult to counter and often force the opposing team to change their entire strategy.

### Emperor Impact (Unique)
Modeled after the legendary Kaiser, this weapon is the undisputed king of offense. When activated, your next shot becomes hyper-powered, making it nearly impossible for average goalkeepers to react in time. Its true strength lies in its versatility; it can be combined with other abilities like Riptide or Power Shot to create the "Magna Shot," a projectile with such velocity and curve that it essentially guarantees a goal if aimed correctly.

### Serpent (Unique)
On the defensive side, Serpent reigns supreme. It features a massive hitbox that can clear balls from a significant distance. In competitive circles, Serpent is often credited with winning tournaments because it allows a single defender to shut down multiple attackers simultaneously. If you are playing a defensive role, this is the ultimate goal for your build.

### Bunnies (Rare)
While only a Rare rarity, Bunnies is essential for competitive scrims. It allows players to dominate the air. In a game where lobs into the box are a primary scoring method, a defender with Bunnies can jump up and uncontestedly win the ball every time. While less "flashy" in public autos, its value in high-level play is immeasurable.

### Immense Speed (Legendary)
This weapon is a favorite for wingers and side-mids. It functions in bursts, gradually building your velocity to a peak where you become a blur on the pitch. It is excellent for recovering defensive positions or launching a fast break. However, because it only lasts about 15 seconds with a limited "max speed" window, timing is everything.

### Black Hole Trap (Epic)
This is the premier "setup" weapon. By trapping the ball instantly, you negate the risk of a messy first touch. Offensively, you can throw the ball up, trap it, and immediately transition into a shot or a pass while the defender is still recovering from their initial challenge.

## B and C Tier: Situational and Niche Picks

These weapons are not necessarily "bad," but they are often outclassed by higher-tier options or require very specific conditions to be effective.

*   **Power Shot (B-Tier):** Great for clearing the ball from the defensive third, but the long charge time makes it a liability in tight offensive spaces.
*   **Perfect Counter (B-Tier):** A high-risk, high-reward unique weapon. It requires the opponent to attempt a steal (M2) at the exact moment you activate it. If successful, you gain I-frames and a speed boost; if they don't bite, the move is wasted.
*   **Metavision (C-Tier):** Despite being a Unique, it is often considered the weakest in its rarity class. It provides a speed boost and shows ball trajectory, but the long cooldown and 30-second duration make it less impactful than raw power or defensive hitboxes.
*   **Direct Shot (C-Tier):** Once a meta staple, Direct Shot has fallen off. It requires a perfect pass from a teammate and precise timing. In the fast-paced 2026 meta, relying on a teammate for your weapon to even activate is often a disadvantage.

## Scrims vs. Autos: Understanding the Difference

A common mistake players make when looking at a **locked 2 tierlist** is ignoring the environment they play in. 

In **Autos**, players are often less coordinated. Weapons like **Monster Lift** or **Stealthy Steps** might actually work because defenders don't know how to time their challenges. However, in **Scrims** (competitive 11v11), these weapons are "terrible." 

Competitive players look for **I-frames** (Invincibility Frames). When your ball glows blue, it cannot be stolen. Weapons like Monster Lift do not provide I-frames, meaning a skilled defender can simply chest-trap the ball away from you mid-animation. This is why Emperor Impact and Serpent are so highly valued—they provide results that skill alone cannot easily overcome.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the best weapon in the current locked 2 tierlist?**

A: For offensive players, **Emperor Impact** is widely considered the best due to its scoring potential. For defensive players, **Serpent** is the top choice because of its massive clearing hitbox.

**Q: Why is Monster Lift ranked so low?**

A: Monster Lift is ranked in the "Terrible" tier because it lacks I-frames. Against experienced players, the animation is easy to read and the ball can be stolen right out of the air, making it a liability in competitive matches.

**Q: Can I combine weapon effects?**

A: Yes. Certain weapons like **Power Shot** and **Riptide Curve** can be used in sequence or stacked with **Emperor Impact** to create enhanced shots with increased speed and unique trajectories.

**Q: Is Metavision worth keeping?**

A: Metavision is a Unique weapon, making it rare, but its actual utility is lower than many Epics and Legendaries. It is best used in "Autos" for the speed boost, but most competitive players will eventually spin off of it for a more specialized offensive or defensive tool.
