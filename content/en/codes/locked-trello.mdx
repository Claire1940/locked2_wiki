## Core Mechanics and the Height System

Before diving into specific builds, every player must understand how physical attributes affect performance. Unlike many other sports games, Locked utilizes a dynamic height system where your character's stature directly influences their physics. Taller players naturally possess more power in their shots and a larger defensive presence, but they sacrifice significant movement speed. Conversely, shorter players are the kings of the midfield and wings, capable of outrunning opponents even if their shots lack long-range lethality.

| Attribute | Short Character (Small) | Tall Character (Large) |
|---|---|---|
| **Movement Speed** | High / Superior | Low / Sluggish |
| **Shot Power** | Low / Requires Proximity | High / Long-range Potential |
| **Hitbox Size** | Small (Harder to hit) | Large (Better for blocking) |
| **Playstyle** | Speedster / Playmaker | Power Striker / Goalie |

> **💡 Tip:** If you are playing a shorter character, focus on "dribbling into the box" before shooting. Your lack of power means you need to be much closer to the goal to ensure the ball gets past the keeper.

## Mastering Traits and Flow State

Traits provide passive or active buffs to your character. While some common traits are often ignored by high-level players, legendary traits can turn the tide of a match. The "Flow" state is a temporary transformation that boosts your stats significantly, usually increasing your hitbox size, speed, or power by 5% to 15%.

### Top Tier Traits
1. **Egoist (Legendary):** Widely considered the best trait for offensive players. It maximizes your individual performance and rewards aggressive play.
2. **Golden Zone (Legendary):** Perfect for shooters who want to increase their accuracy and power from specific areas of the pitch.
3. **Unbreakable (Rare):** A solid choice for defenders, making it harder for opponents to knock you off the ball or break your ankles.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful with your Flow activation. Using it at the wrong time (e.g., when the ball is on the other side of the field) wastes your Ego meter, which takes time to recharge through successful tackles and goals.

## Essential Controls and Dribbling Techniques

To compete at a high level, you must move beyond basic movement. The game rewards precision and timing. If you spam your dribble, the ball will drift too far from your character, allowing defenders to steal it easily with a well-timed right-click.

| Action | Keybind | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Flow Activation** | **G** | Activates your Flow state once the Ego meter is at 100%. |
| **Tackle / Steal** | **Q** | Lunges forward to strip the ball or disrupt an opponent. |
| **Weapon Ability** | **R** | Uses your equipped weapon skill (e.g., Riptide). |
| **Charge Stamina** | **B** | Stand still or move slowly to recover your energy bar. |
| **Active Trait** | **T** | Manually triggers your trait if it is not a passive one. |

### Dribbling and Air Play
For advanced players, "Air Dribbling" is a vital skill. By pressing **R** (Weapon) and then right-clicking to pop the ball up, you can spam left-click to keep the ball in the air. This is particularly effective when surrounded by multiple defenders, as it makes the ball much harder to tackle. A speed-focused character combined with a weapon like **Riptide** allows you to outmaneuver the defense and create scoring opportunities through pure agility.

If you prefer a defensive role, aim for height. A tall character with the **Iron Head** weapon can effectively block shots and dominate the air during corner kicks. ## FAQ

**Q: How do I gain Ego quickly in Locked?**

A: Ego is primarily gained by performing successful actions on the pitch. This includes tackling opponents, scoring goals, and successful dribbles. Conversely, missing easy shots or getting "ankle broken" by an opponent will cause you to lose Ego.

**Q: What is the best weapon for a Goalie?**

A: The **Diver** weapon is currently one of the best choices for goalkeepers. It provides enhanced mobility and reach, allowing you to save shots that would otherwise be out of range. Check the **locked trello** for specific stat breakdowns on its cooldown and jump height.

**Q: Does height really matter that much?**

A: Yes. Height in Locked is a trade-off system. Short players are significantly faster but have very weak shots. Tall players are slow but can shoot from the midfield with enough power to overwhelm a goalie. Most competitive strikers aim for a balanced or slightly shorter height to maintain dribbling control.

**Q: How do I use the locked trello effectively?**

A: Use the Trello to look up the specific percentage boosts of your Flow and Traits. For example, some Flow states give a 5% speed boost while others give 15%. Knowing these numbers helps you decide whether to keep a spin or try for something better.
