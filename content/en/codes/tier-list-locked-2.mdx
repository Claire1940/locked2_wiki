## Understanding Weapon Categories in Locked 2

In Locked 2, weapons are generally categorized by their primary position on the field. While some weapons like Power Shot or Immense Speed can be utilized across multiple roles, their effectiveness often shifts depending on where you are positioned. The meta in 2026 favors weapons that offer either high mobility, "I-frame" (invincibility frame) protection, or massive hitboxes that can disrupt the opponent's flow.

When evaluating the **tier list locked 2**, it is important to consider "stacking." Stacking refers to the ability to combine two weapons to create a more powerful effect. For example, Direct Shot might feel underwhelming on its own, but when paired with a high-mobility weapon, it becomes a tier-defining threat.

> **Warning:** Weapons with long cooldowns, such as Dragon Header, require precise timing. Missing your window can leave your team at a significant disadvantage for over two minutes.

Do not let the rarity fool you. Stylish Reach is frequently cited as the most "insane" weapon currently available. ### The Riptide Learning Curve
Riptide has undergone significant changes in 2026. The "straight" version of the move is gone, replaced by a version with extreme curve. While this makes it harder for goalkeepers to predict, it also makes it less beginner-friendly. If you can master the arc, it sits comfortably in the A-tier, but newer players may find it frustrating to control.

> **💡 Tip:** When using Monster Lift in the midfield, be aware that you have no I-frames. Skilled defenders will wait for the animation to start and easily take the ball from you.

## Advanced Strategies: Stacking and Flow

To truly master the **tier list locked 2**, you must understand how to maximize the Flow state. Flow is a temporary power-up that enhances the properties of your equipped weapons. For instance, Power Shot becomes significantly faster and harder to block during Flow.

1.  **Weapon Stacking:** Some weapons, like Direct Shot, are designed to be used in tandem with others. Using a mobility weapon to enter the box and then immediately triggering Direct Shot can bypass the timing difficulties usually associated with the move.
2.  **I-Frame Management:** Weapons like Formless provide frames of invincibility. Using these at the exact moment a defender attempts a tackle will allow you to phase through them, maintaining your momentum.
3.  **Mogging and Physicality:** Weapons like Body Zone Offense are specialized for "mogging" (physically overpowering) opponents. While situational, they are excellent for Center Backs who need to clear space in a crowded box.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the best overall weapon in the tier list locked 2?**

A: While it depends on your position, **Stylish Reach** is widely considered the best overall weapon due to its massive hitbox and utility in both midfield and defense. For pure offense, **Nagi** and **Power Shot** are the top choices.

**Q: Why is Dragon Header ranked so low?**

A: Dragon Header suffers from a 150-second cooldown, which is the longest in the game. The payoff for the move does not justify the wait time, especially when compared to Crushing Header, which provides better results with more frequent availability.

**Q: Can common weapons really compete with rare ones?**

A: Absolutely. In the current **tier list locked 2**, Stylish Reach (a common) outperforms many rare and legendary weapons because its hitbox mechanics are superior for the current defensive meta.

**Q: How do I-frames work in Locked 2?**

A: I-frames, or invincibility frames, prevent your character from being tackled or losing the ball during specific animations. Weapons like Monster Lift are considered weak because they lack these frames, making them easy to counter.
