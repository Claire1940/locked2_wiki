## Understanding Dribbling Styles

Once you have figured out **how to spawn ball in locked 2**, the next step is keeping it. Locked 2 features two primary dribbling styles: **Beginner** and **Legacy**. Choosing the right one depends on your skill level and your role on the team.

### Beginner Dribbling (The Magnet)
Beginner dribbling is designed for those just starting their journey. Think of your character as a giant magnet and the ball as a metal fork. When you hold your M1 (Left Click), the ball will automatically center itself to your character's position. No matter which direction you move, the ball stays glued to your reactive spot.

While this sounds ideal, it is actually considered one of the least effective methods for high-level play. Because the ball is always centered, it is much easier for a defender to slide in and steal it from your hitbox.

### Legacy Dribbling (Competitive Standard)
Legacy dribbling is the gold standard for competitive Locked 2 players. Unlike the beginner mode, the ball does not automatically center. Instead, it moves based on your directional inputs (W, A, S, D). If you hold M1 and move, the ball will "repel" or travel in the direction you are heading. This allows you to keep the ball on the "edge" of your hitbox, making it significantly harder for defenders to reach.

| Feature | Beginner Dribbling | Legacy Dribbling |
|---|---|---|
| **Ball Positioning** | Automatically centers to player. | Moves based on W, A, S, D inputs. |
| **Difficulty** | Low - Good for learning. | High - Requires precise movement. |
| **Steal Risk** | High - Ball is in the middle of hitbox. | Low - Ball can be kept on the edges. |
| **Competitive Use** | Rarely used in high-ranks. | The mandatory choice for CMs and Forwards. |

## Advanced Ball Control: The Hitbox Edge

One of the most important secrets shared by top players like Ace and Crimson is the "Edge Dribbling" technique. In Locked 2, every player has a hitbox that determines where they can interact with the ball. If you keep the ball in the middle of this box, a defender only needs to get close to trigger a steal.

By using **Legacy Dribbling**, you can push the ball to the very edge of your hitbox. This creates a physical gap between you and the defender. Even if they have "Long Legs" or high reach stats, they cannot M2 (Right Click) the ball if it is positioned correctly on your perimeter.

### How to Master Edge Positioning:
1.  **Enter Legacy Mode:** Ensure your settings are toggled to Legacy.
2.  **Directional Pushing:** While moving forward (W), use slight taps of A or D to nudge the ball to your diagonal front.
3.  **Flicking:** Use M1 to flick the ball up and around opponents. The goal is to keep the ball "revolving" around your edge rather than sitting stationary in your center.

## Stamina Management and the "Down Slam"

You cannot maintain a high-speed dribble if your stamina bar is empty. Locked 2 players have discovered a technique known as the **Down Slam** to manage energy efficiently. This is particularly effective for players using the "Iron Header" or "Riptide" builds.

By slamming the ball down and immediately regaining momentum, you can trick the game's physics to allow for faster stamina recovery while maintaining ball control. 

> **⚠️ Warning:** Mis-timing a down slam can result in the ball bouncing too far away, leaving you vulnerable to a counter-attack. Practice the timing in a private server first.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I change from Beginner to Legacy dribbling?**

A: You can usually toggle your dribbling style within the game's settings menu or by using a specific keybind designated in the controls overlay. Most competitive players keep it on Legacy permanently.

**Q: Why does the ball keep flying away when I try to dribble?**

A: This usually happens if you are in Legacy mode but aren't countering the ball's momentum with your movement keys. In Legacy, the ball "repels" from you. You must move in the same direction you are pushing the ball to maintain control.

**Q: What is the best height for dribbling in Locked 2?**

A: While height is a preference, many top Center Mids (CMs) prefer a height around 6'3". This provides a balanced hitbox that is large enough to reach for flicks but small enough to remain agile.

**Q: How to spawn ball in locked 2 if it gets stuck?**

A: If the ball glitches out of the map or gets stuck in a wall, use the `!reset` or `!ball` command if you have permissions in a private server. In public matches, the ball will typically reset automatically after a short period of inactivity.
