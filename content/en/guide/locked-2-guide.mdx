## Understanding Flow Types and Traits

One of the most significant changes in the 2026 meta is how Flow traits and buffs function. Unlike the simplified systems of the past, your choice of Flow dictates your stamina consumption and the duration of your peak performance. When customizing your character, you will encounter several options, but the current competitive landscape favors specific archetypes.

The two standout options for most players are **Off-ball** and **Burst**. While many players argue over the "best" trait, Off-ball speed is widely considered superior for competitive 11v11 formats. It provides a consistent speed flow that lasts significantly longer because you lose less flow when you aren't actively holding the ball. Conversely, Burst is excellent for high-impact moments but requires specific secondary traits like Reach or Strength to be effective.

| Flow Trait | Primary Benefit | Best Used For | Drain Rate |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Off-ball** | Consistent speed multiplier | 11v11 Scrims, Midfielders | **Low** (when without ball) |
| **Burst** | High explosive power | Forwards, Goal-scoring | **High** |
| **Tenacity** | Weaker buff, longer duration | Defensive anchors | **Very Low** |
| **Technique** | Improved ball handling | Playmakers (Niche) | **Medium** |

> **💡 Tip:** If you are playing in a competitive 11v11 environment, prioritize Off-ball speed. The multiplier is higher than Tenacity, and the duration allows you to stay in the zone for almost the entire half.

## Height Mechanics and Attribute Scaling

In the 2026 version of the game, height is no longer just a cosmetic choice or a minor speed modifier. It is now one of the top two factors that fundamentally change how your character interacts with the ball and the field. Every height comes with specific percentage-based modifiers to your cooldowns, power, and stamina.

For example, a **5'9" build** is considered the "perfect all-rounder" because it offers the lowest cooldowns across the board (100% baseline). If you prefer playing as a striker, a **6'0" build** is often preferred as it opens up options for weapons like Formless or Big Bang Drive while maintaining decent agility.

| Height | Speed Modifier | Power Modifier | Cooldown Efficiency | Recommended Position |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **5'3"** | **Extremely High** | Low | 105% | Winger / Agility Midfield |
| **5'9"** | Balanced | Balanced | **100% (Best)** | All-rounder / CM |
| **6'0"** | Medium | **High** | 110% | Striker / Target Man |
| **6'3"** | Low | **Very High** | 120% | Center Back / Long Passer |

Experimenting with height is crucial. While a YouTuber might suggest a "meta" height, your personal playstyle should dictate your choice. A 6'3" midfielder might be slower, but their reach and power for long passes can dictate the tempo of a game in ways a 5'3" speedster cannot.

## Mastering the Manual Bicycle Kick

A subtle but game-changing addition to the mechanics is the **Manual Bicycle Kick**. Previously tied to specific weapons, this move can now be executed by any player, regardless of their equipped weapon slots. This mechanic allows players to reach heights far beyond their standard jump, making it an essential tool for both offensive headers and defensive clearances.

To perform a bicycle kick, you must hold your charge, jump, and then hold the "S" key (or your backward movement bind) before releasing the kick mid-air. If you release too early, you will perform a standard shot; too late, and you will kick while falling, losing all power.

### Managing the Nagi Conflict
If you are a Nagi weapon user, the manual bicycle kick can actually be a hindrance. Many Nagi players have a habit of holding "S" while shooting to back away from defenders. In the 2026 update, this will trigger a bicycle kick, often flinging the ball backward toward your own team.

> **⚠️ Warning:** If you find the manual bicycle kick interfering with your Nagi muscle memory, go to **Settings > Controls** and disable the "Bicycle Kick" toggle until you are comfortable with the new inputs.

## The Art of Weapon Stacking

Weapon stacking is an intentional mechanic confirmed by the developers for the 2026 season. This allows players to combine the best traits of two different weapons into a single, devastating move. This isn't considered a bug; rather, it is a high-level skill ceiling that separates casual players from the elite.

When you stack weapons, the game applies the most beneficial attributes from both. A common example is stacking **Emperor** with **Riptide**. 
1. **Riptide** provides incredible curve but has a standard charge time.
2. **Emperor** provides an instant charge and a power boost.
3. **The Result:** An instant-charge power shot that curves aggressively, often referred to by the community as the "Magnus Kaiser."

| Combination | Primary Benefit | Synergy Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Emperor + Power Shot** | Deletes the 5-second charge time of Power Shot | **S-Tier** |
| **Emperor + Direct Shot** | Instant, high-velocity volley | **A-Tier** |
| **Formless + Dragon Header** | Extreme verticality and mid-air power | **S-Tier** |
| **Riptide + Emperor** | High-speed curving long shots | **A-Tier** |

## Private Server Commands and Map Customization

For those looking to host scrims or practice sessions, the private server (PS) system has been completely overhauled. Gone are the days of clunky chat commands. By pressing the **M key**, admins can access a dedicated command GUI to manage every aspect of the match.

Admins can now set specific multipliers for ball gravity, power, and hitbox sizes. This is particularly useful for the "Tester World Cup" formats where specific competitive sliders are required. Additionally, maps are no longer free; you must purchase them using in-game Yen earned through daily challenges and matches.

### Essential PS Multipliers (2026 Standard)
*   **Ball Gravity:** Default is **7**. Setting this to 1 will cause the ball to float away, while 20 will make it drop like lead.
*   **Field Size:** You can toggle between **Large** (11v11) and **Small** (7v7/8v8) on the fly.
*   **Flow Charge:** Adjusts how quickly players enter their "Zone."
