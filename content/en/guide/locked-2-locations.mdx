## Training and Weapon Development

One of the most unique aspects of the facility is how it handles player progression and "weapons." In the context of this game, weapons refer to your physical attributes and your "Flow" abilities. To master these, you must visit specific **locked 2 locations** dedicated to player stats.

### The Examination Room
The Examination Room is arguably the most important area for players looking to understand their power ceiling. Here, you can visualize your physical weapons and Flow. The room displays various legendary and unique items that represent the peak of player performance.

*   **Physical Weapons:** These represent your raw athletic stats like speed and strength.
*   **Flow:** This represents your "zone" or special abilities that can be triggered during intense gameplay.
*   **Legendary Displays:** Use these to compare your current build against the highest-tier items in the game.

### The Gym
While the **Gym** is one of the most visually impressive **locked 2 locations**, it is currently primarily an aesthetic area. As of early 2026, the gym consists of high-quality models and objects that represent a training environment, but it does not yet feature active training scripts. However, it serves as an excellent spot for roleplay or meeting up with clan members.

| Category | Available Items | Currency Used |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful when purchasing Map Packs; ensure you are in a party that intends to play those specific maps to get the most value out of your purchase.

## Navigation and Matchmaking Logic

Getting into a game involves more than just clicking a menu button. The **Matchmaking Lobby** is a physical space among the **locked 2 locations** where players must congregate. 

To join a match, you typically need to find a seat or enter a designated zone within the lobby. Once seated, a small User Interface (UI) will appear, allowing you to select your preferred game mode and queue up. This immersive approach to matchmaking is a staple of the experience, making the facility feel like a living, breathing world.

### Floor 2 and the Lobby Loop
The facility is divided into multiple floors. If you find yourself on **Floor 2**, you can easily return to the main hub by clicking the "Lobby" button in your navigation menu. This will teleport you back to the main spawn area. 

There are also several "Dead Zones" or aesthetic rooms, such as the **Data Monitor Room**. Much like the Gym, the Data Monitor Room is currently an object-based environment. While it looks like a high-tech command center, there are no functional scripts for players to interact with at this time. It is mostly used to look out over the "outside" world of the facility.

## Exploring the Outer Perimeter

For those who enjoy exploring the boundaries of the map, the game allows you to walk quite far from the central hub. However, players should be aware that there is no automatic respawn if you wander too far into the empty zones surrounding the facility. 

If you find yourself stuck in the void or too far from the main **locked 2 locations**, the most efficient way to return is to use the "Reset Character" option in the Roblox menu or use the "Return to Lobby" UI element if available. 

1.  **Check your coordinates:** If the facility is no longer visible, you have gone too far.
2.  **Use the UI:** The "Lobby" button is your best friend for quick navigation.
3.  **Avoid Dead Ends:** Areas like the Data Monitor room offer great views but no exits to the exterior.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find the store in Locked 2?**

A: The store is located within the **Conference Room**. Look for the large table and monitors; interacting with this area will open the menu to buy items, maps, and emotes.

**Q: Why can't I interact with the equipment in the Gym?**

A: Currently, the Gym is an aesthetic location composed of models and objects. It does not have active training scripts yet, though it remains one of the most popular **locked 2 locations** for player meetups.

**Q: How do I change my character's clothes?**

A: You need to go to the **Locker Room**. This area is specifically designed for customization and allows you to change your appearance and equipped gear.

**Q: What should I do if I get lost outside the main facility?**

A: Since there is no auto-respawn for wandering too far, you should use the "Lobby" button in your UI to instantly teleport back to the main spawn point.
