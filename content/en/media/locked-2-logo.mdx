Unlike the first game, where symbols were largely static, the logos in the sequel are dynamic. They may only appear under specific lighting conditions or when the player has certain items equipped, such as the UV Flashlight or the Signal Scanner.

Understanding the visual cues associated with the **locked 2 logo** is vital for progression. ## How to Find the Hidden Logos in Chapter 1

Chapter 1 introduces the player to the basic mechanics of the game. While most players focus on finding the keycard for the main elevator, completionists should look for the first three instances of the logo hidden within the administrative offices.

The first **locked 2 logo** is located behind the reception desk. You must move the rolling chair to reveal a small floor safe. Once the safe is opened (using the code found on the calendar), the logo is revealed on the interior lid. Collecting this grants the "First Impression" achievement.

The second logo requires a bit more technical finesse. You will need to locate the server room and interact with the primary terminal. By navigating the file directory to the "Archives" folder, you can trigger a display that renders the logo across four different monitors.

> **💡 Tip:** If you are having trouble seeing the digital logos, check your in-game brightness settings. Some logos are designed to be "invisible" if your gamma is set too high, mimicking the way light reflects off real-world security hardware.

## Advanced Mechanics: Passage Mode and Connectivity

One of the most innovative features in Locked 2 is the "Passage Mode" mechanic. When you find a **locked 2 logo** that is glowing blue, it signifies that the local area can be temporarily de-synchronized. By pressing the interaction button, you enter a state where the "auto lock" on all nearby doors is disabled. This is essential for timed puzzles where you must move heavy objects between rooms that would otherwise lock behind you.

Furthermore, the game utilizes a "Frequency Tuning" system that mimics real-world wireless bands. Some logos are hidden behind "interference." To reveal them, you must use your handheld scanner to find a 2.4 GHz signal. If your scanner is set to the wrong band (such as 5 GHz or 6 GHz), the logo will remain a blurred smudge on the wall.

## Troubleshooting Logo Visibility Issues

If you are following this guide but cannot find the **locked 2 logo** at the specified coordinates, you may be experiencing a "State Conflict." Locked 2 uses a persistent world system where certain actions in previous chapters can "lock out" content in later ones. 

Common issues include:
1. **Bluetooth Interference:** In the Research Lab level, if you haven't disabled the internal security Bluetooth hub, the digital logos will not render on the tablets.
2. **Battery Life:** Your UV Flashlight must be at least 50% charged to reveal the Spectral Glow logos.
3. **Firmware Updates:** Ensure your in-game "OS" is updated at a save terminal. Older versions of the protagonist's software cannot decode the Glitch Variant logos.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Attempting to force-open a door marked with a red logo will trigger an immediate "Game Over" via the facility's automated defense system. Always look for the blue "Passage Mode" variant before attempting to bypass security.

## Solving the Final Logo Puzzle

The final **locked 2 logo** is located in the heart of the Reactor Core. Unlike previous versions, this one is massive and integrated into the floor's geometry. To "collect" it, you must align the overhead cranes to cast a shadow that completes the logo's shape. This requires a deep understanding of the game's light-and-shadow physics engine.

Once the shadow is aligned, the floor will descend, revealing the final boss arena. Players who have collected all previous logos will find that the boss has 15% less health, as the "knowledge" gained from the logos acts as a debuff against the facility's central AI.

## FAQ

**Q: Why is the locked 2 logo changing colors in my game?**

A: The logo color indicates the type of interaction required. Blue means "Passage Mode" is available, Red means the area is currently dead-bolted and requires a key, and Green means the puzzle has been successfully solved.

**Q: Can I find the locked 2 logo on a mobile device?**

A: While there is a companion app for Locked 2 on iOS and Android, the actual collectible logos are only found within the main PC and console versions of the game. The mobile app is primarily used for decoding signals found near the logos.

**Q: Is the 2.4 GHz frequency requirement a real-world thing?**

A: In the context of the game's puzzles, yes. The developers added this as a "tech tip" style puzzle to make the hacking feel more grounded in reality. You must manually tune your in-game scanner to 2.4 GHz to see certain "hidden" Wi-Fi logos.

**Q: What happens if I miss a logo in Chapter 1?**

A: You can use the "Chapter Select" feature from the main menu after beating the game once. However, for your first playthrough, missed logos cannot be retrieved once you enter the "Point of No Return" in Chapter 5.
