## Character Customization and Physical Stats

One of the most significant **locked 2 all changes** involves how character builds function. In the original game, physical attributes were largely cosmetic. In Locked 2, your height and physical build now directly influence your performance on the field.

## Gameplay Mechanics and Physics Overhaul

The core gameplay has seen a massive shift in philosophy. The developers have moved toward a "Legacy" vs "Beginner" control scheme to accommodate different skill levels. 

*   **Beginner Style:** This mode uses a "magnetic" ball mechanic. When you click, the ball is pulled toward your character, making it easier for new players to maintain control without precise directional inputs.
*   **Legacy Style:** This is the classic Locked 1 dribbling. It is entirely based on your character's movement and camera orientation. Competitive players generally prefer this for the higher ceiling of control it offers.

### Tackling and Eye-Frames
The tackle system (Q) has been completely reworked. In the previous game, a successful tackle would latch the ball to your legs and grant a set number of eye-frames (invincibility frames). In Locked 2, the ball no longer sticks to your body. You must now manually dribble and create space after a successful tackle. Furthermore, self-tackling your own ball provides fewer eye-frames than tackling an opponent, reducing the "eye-frame abuse" that was prevalent in the past.

### The Header Meta
Headers are significantly stronger in 2026, but they now come with a dedicated cooldown to prevent spamming. The "Header Up" mechanic from the first game has been replaced by a "Header Trap." Instead of launching the ball vertically for an infinite air dribble, the ball now performs a controlled touch, making it harder to stay airborne indefinitely.

## New Skill-Based Moves

Several new mechanics have been added to the base kit of every player, regardless of their equipped weapons.

1.  **Manual Bicycle Kick:** You can now perform a bicycle kick without a specific weapon. While it is weaker than weapon-based shots and has difficult timing, it is an excellent tool for mid-air interceptions and surprise shots.
2.  **Body Faint:** By holding right-click to charge power and then left-clicking, your character performs a pump fake. This is designed to juke goalkeepers or hesitant defenders.
3.  **Tap Lifts:** Looking straight down and tapping the dribble button results in a small, quick lift. This is useful for maintaining momentum or setting up specific weapon skills like an "Insta-Formless" shot.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful with the Bicycle Kick toggle in your settings. If enabled, holding the "S" key while shooting might trigger a bicycle kick accidentally, potentially sending the ball toward your own goal.

For those who run community tournaments or private practice sessions, the command system has been modernized. By typing `/cmd`, players can access a GUI that handles everything from resetting flows to clearing balls from the field.

## FAQ

**Q: How do the new Locked 2 all changes affect air dribbling?**

A: Air dribbling has been significantly nerfed. The new physics cause the ball's momentum to drop sharply after a chest bump, and the "Header Up" move has been replaced by a trap that keeps the ball closer to the ground. This makes sustained flight much more difficult than in the first game.

**Q: What is the best height for a character in Locked 2?**

A: As of the current 2026 meta, many players prefer a height of 6'0". This provides a sweet spot that offers reduced weapon cooldowns without sacrificing too much reach or mobility. However, your ideal height may vary based on your role.

**Q: Can I still stack weapons in Locked 2?**

A: Weapon stacking still exists, but it has been balanced. Rather than creating "broken" speed combinations, stacking now typically allows you to benefit from the best traits of both weapons, such as combining the curve of Riptide with the instant charge of Emperor.

**Q: What is the difference between Legacy and Beginner dribbling?**

A: Beginner dribbling uses a "magnetic" effect to keep the ball near you when you click, which is great for learning the ropes. Legacy dribbling is the classic style from the first game, offering more precise control for experienced players who want to use their movement to manipulate the ball's path.
