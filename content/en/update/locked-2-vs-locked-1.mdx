The matchmaking dynamics have shifted as the player base matures. While 4v4 remains the "gold standard" for competitive play, it often suffers from longer queue times compared to 1v1 and 2v2 modes. ### 1v1 and 2v2: The Dribbler's Playground
These modes are perfect for testing new weapon combinations. Since you have more space on the pitch, you can practice your dribbling styles and learn how to "break ankles" (stunning the opponent with a well-timed move).

## Mastering the "Flow" State

A mechanic that has been greatly expanded in Locked 2 is the "Flow" state. Unlike the original game where "going into flow" felt like a generic power-up, Locked 2 offers specialized Flow abilities. Activating Flow requires consistent performance—successful passes, steals, and shots build your meter. Once active, your character's physical presence on the field changes, often accompanied by unique visual effects that signal to the opposition that you are at your peak.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not activate Flow the moment you get it. Save it for a critical breakaway or a final defensive stand in the closing seconds of a match.
