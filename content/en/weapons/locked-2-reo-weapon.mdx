## Understanding Chameleon Copy Mechanics

The core of the Chameleon Copy weapon lies in its situational adaptability. Unlike static weapons that provide a single set of skills, the Chameleon Copy is entirely dependent on the players present in your match. It functions as a "Copycat" ability that scans the field and replicates a weapon currently equipped by another player.

The most important factor to remember is that the skill you receive is determined by ball possession at the exact moment you activate the ability. This creates two distinct modes of play:

### Offensive Mode
If you have individual possession of the ball (meaning the ball is at your feet, not just with your team), activating the weapon will grant you an offensive ability. This could be anything from a high-power strike to a sophisticated dribbling maneuver, provided someone else on the field has that weapon equipped.

### Defensive Mode
If you do not have possession of the ball, activating the weapon will grant you a defensive or utility ability. This is perfect for intercepting passes, blocking shots, or catching up to fast-moving strikers.

| Mode | Possession Status | Weapon Type Obtained | Best Use Case |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Offensive** | You have the ball | **Attacking/Striker** | Scoring goals, breaking ankles, long shots |
| **Defensive** | Opponent/Teammate has ball | **Defensive/Midfield** | Stealing the ball, blocking, positioning |

> **💡 Tip:** Always check the leaderboard or observe your opponents' moves early in the match. Since you can only copy what is present on the field, knowing who has which weapon allows you to time your activation for the specific skill you need.

## FAQ

**Q: Is the locked 2 reo weapon better than Nagi's weapon?**

A: It depends on your playstyle. Nagi's weapon is superior for specialized ball control and trapping, but the Reo weapon offers much higher versatility. If you prefer to adapt to the game's flow, Reo is the better choice.

**Q: Can I copy a weapon if the other player hasn't used it yet?**

A: Yes. As long as the other player has the weapon equipped in their loadout for that match, it is added to the "pool" of weapons that Chameleon Copy can pull from.

**Q: Does the Chameleon Copy copy the stats of the other player?**

A: No, it only copies the weapon's active and passive abilities. Your base stats (speed, power, stamina) remain your own. This makes it important to have a balanced stat build to support different types of copied moves.

**Q: What happens if I use the weapon and no one else has a weapon equipped?**

A: In the rare case that no other players have weapons (such as in a beginner lobby), the Chameleon Copy will default to a basic set of moves or fail to activate. However, in standard play, this is almost never an issue.
