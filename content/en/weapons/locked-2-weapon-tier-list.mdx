## Locked 2 Weapon Tier List: S-Tier Breakdown

The S-Tier represents the absolute pinnacle of the meta. These weapons are game-changers that offer immense utility, high scoring potential, or impenetrable defense. If you roll one of these, you should almost never spin off of it unless your specific team role demands a very niche alternative.

| Weapon | Type | Primary Role | Why It’s S-Tier |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Emperor Impact** | Unique | Striker / Winger | Hyper-powered shots that are nearly impossible to block. |
| **Serpent** | Unique | Center Back / Defender | Massive defensive hitbox that clears almost any shot. |
| **Bunnies** | Rare | Defender / CDM | Dominates aerial balls; essential for winning lobs in scrims. |
| **Big Bang Drive** | Unique | Midfield / Attack | Versatile shot that requires aim but offers massive rewards. |

### Emperor Impact
Emperor Impact remains the gold standard for offensive play. Based on the legendary Kaiser from the manga, this weapon ensures your next shot is hyper-powered. In 2026, it is frequently paired with other abilities like Riptide or Power Shot to create "Magna Shots," which carry enough force to blow past even the most skilled goalkeepers. It is equally effective in autos and competitive play.

### Serpent
If you are playing defense, Serpent is your holy grail. Its hitbox is notoriously large, allowing you to intercept shots and passes that would otherwise be out of reach. It was a deciding factor in the 2026 Content Creator Cup, proving that a team with multiple Serpent users can effectively "clamp" any offense.

### Bunnies
While only a Rare weapon, Bunnies is indispensable in the competitive 11v11 scene. When a ball is lobbed into the box, a player using Bunnies will almost always win the contest. This creates uncontested shot opportunities or vital defensive clears. While less "flashy" in public autos, its value in coordinated play is unmatched.

## A-Tier: High-Performance Alternatives

A-Tier weapons are incredibly strong and can often compete with S-Tier options depending on your personal playstyle. These are reliable picks for both competitive and casual play in the current **locked 2 weapon tier list** meta.

| Weapon | Rarity | Best Use Case |
|---|---|---|
| **Black Hole Trap** | Epic | Offense; jumping over defenders for free shots. |
| **Immense Speed** | Legendary | Wingers/Fullbacks; catching loose balls and dribbling. |
| **Crushing Header** | Epic | Aerial offense; creates Emperor-level power from headers. |
| **Riptide Curve** | Legendary | Creating unpredictable shot trajectories. |

### Immense Speed
Immense Speed provides bursts of velocity that allow you to outpace defenders or track back to stop a counter-attack. While it only lasts for about 15 seconds and requires a charge-up period, the utility it provides for Midfielders and Fullbacks is immense. When combined with Metavision, it becomes a nightmare for the opposing team to track.

### Black Hole Trap
This weapon is a favorite for technical strikers. It auto-locks the ball to your feet, allowing you to trap lobs instantly. This is perfect for setting up a Nagi-style play where you trap the ball, jump over a sliding defender, and fire an unblockable shot into the top corner.

> **💡 Tip:** In Scrims, use Black Hole Trap to bait a defender's M2 (steal attempt), then use the trap's positioning to jump over them for a clear line of sight to the goal.

## B and C Tier: Situational and Niche Picks

Weapons in these tiers are either too specialized or have been power-crept by newer abilities. While they aren't "bad" per se, they often require more effort to achieve the same results as higher-tier weapons.

### Stylish Reach (B-Tier)
Stylish Reach is a fantastic defensive tool that increases your tackle hitbox. However, its ranking is limited because it is strictly defensive. If an attacker uses this, they are effectively wasting their weapon slot. It is highly effective for Left Backs and Right Backs who need to stop wingers from crossing the ball.

### Power Shot (B-Tier)
Power Shot can be devastating when stacked with Emperor Impact, but on its own, the charge time is a major liability. In high-level play, defenders will rarely give you the 2-3 seconds required to fully charge this shot. It is best used by CDMs to clear the ball deep into the opponent's half.

### Metavision (C-Tier)
Despite being a Unique rarity, Metavision is often considered the weakest of its class in 2026. It provides a speed boost and shows ball trajectory, but the long cooldown and short duration make it less impactful than raw power or defensive hitboxes. It shines most in "Autos" where players are less coordinated.

## Specialized Roles: Goalkeepers and Wildcards

Goalkeeping in Locked 2 is a different beast entirely. Most standard weapons are useless in the box, which is why specific GK weapons exist.

*   **Primal Handstand:** This is widely considered the best GK weapon. Its hitbox is so massive that a keeper can stand in the center of the goal and block "top bin" shots without moving. It is so powerful that many competitive leagues have rules restricting its use to only the designated goalkeeper.
*   **Bear Claw:** A solid Common-tier GK weapon. If you are a dedicated keeper and don't have the funds to roll for Primal, Bear Claw is a mandatory baseline.
*   **Chameleon (The Wildcard):** Chameleon is a Legendary weapon that copies a random offensive or defensive ability from another player on the field. It is the definition of "high risk, high reward." You could end up with Emperor Impact or you could end up with a useless speed boost.

## Understanding Game Mechanics in 2026

To truly utilize a **locked 2 weapon tier list**, you must understand the underlying mechanics that make these weapons work.

### I-Frames (Invincibility Frames)
When the ball glows blue during a move (like Perfect Counter or certain dashes), you have I-frames. This means the ball cannot be stolen from you by a defender's M2. Weapons like **Monster Lift** are ranked low specifically because they lack these frames, making them easy to "chest trap" or steal mid-animation.

### Scrims vs. Autos
*   **Autos:** Focus on solo-carry weapons. Emperor Impact and Immense Speed are king here because you cannot always rely on teammates to pass.
*   **Scrims:** Focus on team utility. Serpent and Bunnies become significantly more valuable because winning the ball for your team is more important than individual scoring.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the best weapon for a beginner in Locked 2?**

A: If you are just starting, **Emperor Impact** is the most straightforward and powerful weapon to aim for. It simplifies the scoring process. If you prefer defense, **Serpent** or **Stylish Reach** are great for learning how to time your tackles.

**Q: Can I combine two different weapons?**

A: Yes, certain weapons allow for "stacking." For example, using **Riptide Curve** and **Emperor Impact** together can create a shot that is both hyper-powered and follows a curved trajectory, making it nearly impossible for a goalkeeper to predict.

**Q: Why is Monster Lift ranked so low on the locked 2 weapon tier list?**

A: Monster Lift lacks I-frames (invincibility frames). When you activate the move, a skilled defender can simply jump up and take the ball from you mid-air. Against experienced players, this weapon becomes a liability rather than an asset.

**Q: How do I get more money to roll for better weapons?**

A: Money is earned by playing matches (Autos) and completing daily challenges. Winning matches provides a significant bonus. Many players also keep an eye out for promotional codes released by the developers, which often grant free spins or cash.
